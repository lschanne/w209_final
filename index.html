Introduction

Brazil hosts the largest Tropical Rain forest in the world with 30% of the
world tropical forests residing within it. The country with the second largest
portion, Congo, only has 8% of the world's tropical forest. The Amazon Rain Forest
is home to a diverse ecosystem which is important to local communities as well as
the wider ecosystems and climate around the world. While it is an integral part of
a global ecosystem, it is at risk of degradation due to deforestation over time.

Deforestation by Year

The Brazil Amazon Rainforest has been experiencing deforestation since
the 1960s. It reached a peak high on 2004, and an effort to Reduce Emission
from Deforestation and Forest Degradation (REDD+) was proposed in 2005.

Deforestation Over Time

While REDD+ proved to help reduce deforestation in Brazil in the short
term, there has been an increase in deforested area starting 2013. From
2012 to 2020 deforested area has increased by 114%.




Total Deforestation

Within the last 16 years, a total of 171,223 km² of the Brazillian Amazon
Rainforest has been degraded due to deforestation. The total deforested
area is similar in size to the following countries, Tunisia, Uruguay, Cambodia
as well as the state of Florida.

Major Causes and Impact of Brazillian
Amazon Rainforest Degredation

The main causes that lead to deforestation in the Brazillian Amazon
Rainforest are stated as agricultural expansion, wood extraction, and
infrastructure expansion by NASA.

Among the major causes, agriculture is the highest contributor. In
comparison with other factors, Land-Use stands out. It is a good
example of the REDD+ policy in action as after its proposal, a
drastic decline in Land-Use is show cased. When included it within
total GHG emission metric it seems as if Brazil does not have an
issue with GHG emissions.

However, when we exclude Land-Use, Agricultre can be highlighted
as the highest contributer which has beeen steadily growing. This
also fits in line with the deforested area growth.

Agriculture and Fires

Agricutlure as a contributor is concerning due to a frequent method used
to clear land to plant crops and raise livestock is to burn parts of
the forest. This method is referred to as the Slash-and-Burn method.
Major fires data collected by the National Institute for Space Research
(INPE) can be linked to deforested area over time.


Soy Beans as an Export

Brazil is the world leading producer of Soybean with steady
production increase in the past few decades. The world's demand
for Soybean is increasing leading to increase in deforestation
rates due to dangerous farming methods, namely Slash-and-Burn.
